RASGULLA

  • Ingredients:


    • > 1 liter whole milk
    > 2 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar (for curdling)
    > 1 cup sugar
    > 4 cups water
    > 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder (optional)
    > 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)
    > Chopped pistachios or almonds (for garnish, optional)

  • Instructions:

    • Step 1: Prepare the Chenna


    > Boil the milk in a heavy-bottomed pot. Stir occasionally to prevent it from scorching.
    > Once the milk comes to a boil, add the lemon juice or vinegar gradually while stirring. The milk will curdle, and the curds (chenna) will separate from the whey.
    > Remove from heat and strain the curds using a muslin or cheese cloth. Rinse under cold water to remove the acidic taste.
    > Squeeze out excess water and let the chenna sit for about 30 minutes.

    Step 2: Make the Rasgulla


    > Knead the chenna for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and forms a dough-like consistency.
    > Divide the chenna into small equal portions and roll them into smooth balls (about the size of a marble).

    Step 3: Prepare the Sugar Syrup


    > In a pot, combine sugar and water. Bring it to a boil, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely.
    > Once the syrup is boiling, gently add the chenna balls to the syrup. Make sure thereâ€™s enough space for them to expand.

    Step 4: Cook the Rasgulla


    > Cover the pot and let the rasgullas cook in the syrup for about 15-20 minutes. They will double in size.
    > Once cooked, add cardamom powder and rose water (if using) to the syrup for flavor.

    Step 5: Serve


    > Let the rasgullas cool in the syrup.

    Garnish with chopped pistachios or almonds if desired.
